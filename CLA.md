# Contributor License Agreement Policy

This repository is open to community contributions, but non-trivial outside contributions are only merged if the contributor agrees to this policy.

The goal is simple:

- keep contribution provenance clear
- preserve the ability to relicense, dual-license, commercialize, or transfer the project later
- avoid ambiguity if the project grows beyond a single maintainer

## Lightweight Process

For now, maintainers may accept this policy in one of these ways:

- the contributor checks the CLA box in the pull request and leaves an explicit comment that they agree to this policy
- the contributor provides a separately signed copy if a maintainer requests one

Maintainers may ask for a separate signed copy before merge, especially for larger contributions.

## What Contributors Grant

By contributing code, documentation, tests, assets, or other material to this repository and affirming agreement to this policy, the contributor represents that they have the right to submit the contribution and grants the repository owner and any future owner or maintainer of this project:

- a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, irrevocable license to use, reproduce, modify, prepare derivative works of, distribute, sublicense, relicense, publicly display, publicly perform, and otherwise exploit the contribution as part of this project or derivative works
- permission to use the contribution in open-source, source-available, commercial, hosted, private, dual-licensed, or relicensed versions of the project
- a patent license, to the extent the contributor has the right to grant it, for patent claims necessarily infringed by the contribution itself or by combining that contribution with this project

## What Contributors Keep

Unless there is a separate written agreement, contributors keep ownership of their own copyright.

This policy is a license grant to the project and its future maintainers, not an automatic copyright assignment.

## What Contributors Promise

Contributors agree that:

1. they wrote the contribution themselves, or otherwise have the legal right to submit it
2. the contribution does not knowingly violate another party's intellectual property rights
3. they are legally entitled to grant the rights described in this policy
4. if they are contributing on behalf of an employer or client, they have any required permission to do so

## Small Fixes

Maintainers may treat clearly trivial changes, such as typo fixes or formatting-only edits, with lighter process. Even then, maintainers can still request explicit CLA confirmation before merge.

## Different Terms

If a contributor needs different terms, they should contact the maintainers before opening a pull request.
